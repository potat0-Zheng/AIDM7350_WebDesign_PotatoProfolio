Say My Name

My name is Mr. Potato. This is my dirt patch. This is my little mound of soil. This is my life. I’ve been sprouting for months now—I’ve lost count of the days because potatoes don’t wear watches. In less than a year, I’ll be dug up, sliced, deep-fried, mashed, or simply boiled into oblivion. Of course I don’t know that yet. And in a way, I’m already cooked. Look at me, lying here in the damp dark, slowly going moldy. The only thing I can still do is push out a pathetic green sprout—that’s the highlight of my day. From here on out, it’s all downhill. Literally. That’s my “wife” over there—the one already turning black and squishy. She used to be firm and round; now she’s just full of resentment. She keeps saying, “Look at you, you never try. You just sit in the dirt daydreaming.” She’s right. I’m a complete waste of starch.
